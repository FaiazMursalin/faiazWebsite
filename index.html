About Me

Hello, I am S M Faiaz Mursalin, a Computer Science graduate student at Missouri State University specializing in Data Science, with a strong focus on robotics and intelligent systems. My work lies at the intersection of robotics, machine learning, and computer vision, where I aim to design systems that can make adaptive decisions in dynamic, real-world environments.

My current research explores topology-guided adaptive social navigation, where I integrate geometric reasoning with learning-based strategies to enable robots to navigate efficiently in environments shared with humans. More broadly, I am interested in combining structured mathematical representations with data-driven approaches to build robust and scalable autonomous systems.

Alongside my research, I have worked as a Graduate Teaching Assistant, Research Assistant, Data Science Intern, and Software Engineer. These experiences have allowed me to bridge theory and practice—from developing machine learning models for real-world applications to building scalable backend systems using Java Spring Boot and Python Django.

I am currently seeking opportunities in research and industry where I can contribute to solving complex problems in robotics, AI, and data science while continuing to grow as a researcher and engineer.

I completed my bachelor’s degree in Computer Science and Engineering from BRAC University. Outside of work, I enjoy playing football and basketball, traveling, and exploring new cities and hiking trails— experiences that keep me curious and grounded beyond the lab.
